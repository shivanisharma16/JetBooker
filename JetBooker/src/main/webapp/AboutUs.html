An Airline Reservation System (ARS) is a software application that helps airlines manage their passenger bookings and flight schedules. The system helps airlines to automate and streamline their operations, making the booking process faster, more efficient, and cost-effective. With the rise of online travel agencies and e-ticketing, ARS has become an indispensable tool for airlines to manage their reservations and keep pace with the changing demands of the market.

An ARS typically consists of several modules, including a flight search engine, a booking engine, a passenger management module, and a payment gateway. The flight search engine allows passengers to search for flights based on their preferred departure and arrival dates, time, and destination. The booking engine then enables passengers to select and book their preferred flight and seat. The passenger management module helps airlines to manage passenger information, including contact details, flight itineraries, and baggage allowances. The payment gateway enables passengers to make secure online payments using a variety of payment methods, including credit cards, debit cards, and net banking.

To make the ARS more accessible and user-friendly, airlines often integrate it with their websites, allowing passengers to book flights and manage their reservations directly from the airline's website.
